1st Chakra · Safety

Root Chakra: when life feels like a threat.

The Root Chakra represents your need for safety, stability, and survival. When it is balanced, you feel solid ground to act on. When it falls out of balance, fear starts driving your decisions.

Core needSafety
In balanceStability to act
Out of balanceFear, control, or paralysis

The problem is not needing safety. The problem is when your system believes safety is never enough, and starts trading movement for survival.

Most common problems

Do any of these situations sound familiar?

Before talking about the chakra, The Soul Game starts with what you actually live day to day. The Root shows up when the themes of safety, money, the body, home, the future, and stability start taking up too much space in your mind.

🏦

Financial anxiety

You feel like you never have enough safety, even when your situation improves.

🚪

Fear of change

Switching jobs, ending a cycle, or starting something new feels more threatening than it should.

🧊

Paralysis

You know what you should do, but you can't act because the risk feels too big.

⏳

Procrastination

You put off important decisions, waiting to feel more certain, more prepared, or more secure.

🛡️

Excessive control

Any surprise creates tension. You try to control everything so nothing catches you off guard.

⚠️

Constant alert

Even when everything seems fine, your body keeps scanning for the next problem.

The need underneath

What do all these problems have in common?

They are all different attempts to fill the same need: safety. Your system is not trying to sabotage you. It is trying to protect you with old strategies.

Positive and negative form

Every need can be filled in two ways.

The need for safety does not have to be eliminated. It needs to be filled in a way that creates growth instead of suffering.

Healthy form

  • Building savings and financial organization.
  • Planning without becoming a hostage to the plan.
  • Acting with calculated risks.
  • Building habits and a basic routine.
  • Developing confidence in handling surprises.

Negative form

  • Controlling everything out of fear.
  • Avoiding any risk or change.
  • Freezing up in front of decisions.
  • Depending on others to feel safe.
  • Mistaking discomfort for real danger.

Starter protocol

What to practice when the Root fires up?

The goal is not to eliminate fear. It is to build capacities that let you act even when fear shows up.

1

Separate fear from reality

Ask: "is this real danger or the discomfort of change?" A dysregulated Root confuses the two.

2

Create minimum safety

Before demanding courage, lower the risk: a basic plan, whatever savings you can manage, a small next step, the right support.

3

Record the evidence

After acting, write it down: "I did it, I felt afraid, I survived." The system needs real proof to update the pattern.
