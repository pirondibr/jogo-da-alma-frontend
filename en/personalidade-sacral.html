🔸 Vibrational Profile

The 3x amplifier

Every human being has 7 chakras. But 1 to 3 of them are dominant, and they work like a 3x amplifier over everything they represent. If your dominant chakra is the Sacral, that means everything tied to pleasure, creativity, and socializing gets amplified in you: the needs, the strengths, the fears, the biases.

You didn't choose to be this way. This is your natural frequency. And the point isn't to change who you are, it's to align your life with who you already are.

Your strengths, amplified by 3x

🎨

Creativity and dynamism

You create naturally, with your hands, with ideas, with energy. Design, art, cooking, entertainment: wherever there's room for creative expression, you shine.

🎉

A gift for entertaining and connecting

People gravitate toward you because you make everything lighter and more fun. You set the mood. Wherever you are, the energy shifts.

🔄

Adaptability

New roles, different settings, unexpected challenges: it's all stimulation. You adapt fast because novelty is your fuel.

⚡

High energy when stimulated

When something sparks your interest, your energy is contagious. You produce, connect, and create at a pace few can keep up with.

Your shadows, amplified by 3x

The shadows aren't flaws. They're the same strengths running in the negative, when the amplifier distorts instead of empowers.

🌊

Intense emotional instability

Your emotions swing between extremes. One day the world is amazing; the next, everything is unbearable. The roller coaster never stops.

💤

Chronic lack of discipline

When the novelty wears off and it becomes routine, the interest dies. Abandoned projects pile up, not out of laziness, but out of destructive boredom.

💣

Impulsiveness

You act before you think. You react emotionally first, rationalize later. Big decisions made in the heat of the moment.

🚫

Abandoning projects

You start with explosive enthusiasm, but once the work turns into routine, boredom sets in, and with it the escape into the next new project.

👁 The Bias

The filter that distorts everything

"I need something new."

This is the Creative's amplified bias. It works like an invisible filter that distorts how you see and handle problems:

  • You flee routine even when persistence would pay off
  • You mistake boredom for a sign that something is wrong
  • You abandon good paths because they stopped being exciting
  • You chase novelty as anesthesia for the discomfort of staying

The bias isn't a mistake, it's protection. But when it runs unconsciously, it makes you start over forever without ever reaping what you planted.

🏠 Frequency

Your ideal environment

Every environment has a dominant frequency. When the environment's frequency matches your dominant chakras, you flourish. When it's the opposite, you wither, and develop symptoms like anxiety, depression, or chronic anger.

Aligned

You flourish when

  • The environment is dynamic and creative
  • There's freedom to experiment
  • Roles change and evolve
  • Socializing is part of the work

Examples: startups, creative agencies, tourism, entertainment

Neutral

Works, but without the spark

  • Some variety but a lot of structure
  • A sense that something's missing: "it should be more lively"
Misaligned

You wither when

  • The environment is bureaucratic and rigid
  • The tasks are repetitive and predictable
  • There's no room for socializing
  • Routine is the absolute norm

Examples: bureaucratic public sector, solitary and repetitive work

⚖ Self-Demand

The right yardstick for you

Most of the suffering from self-demand comes from measuring yourself with the wrong yardstick. If you're a Creative and you hold yourself to the standard of a Traditional or an Intellectual, you'll feel permanently out of place, not because you are, but because the yardstick isn't yours.

Worth holding yourself to

  • Finishing what you start
  • The minimum discipline to bring ideas to life
  • Not abandoning something good just because it became routine
  • Giving persistence time to produce a result

NOT worth holding yourself to

  • Keeping a rigid, constant routine
  • Being predictable and methodical
  • Having steady energy all the time
  • Being organized like an analytical type
You don't need to be more disciplined. You need creative discipline: a structure flexible enough not to suffocate you, but firm enough to carry you to the finish.
🔑 Formula

The Creative's success formula

Success doesn't mean the same thing to everyone. What makes a Traditional thrive destroys a Creative, and the other way around. Here's your formula:

What works for you

Freedom Constant variety Creative expression Social connections Dynamic projects A stimulating environment

What will frustrate you

A fixed routine A rigid role Solitary work Inflexible structure Total predictability A lack of stimulation

This doesn't mean you can never have structure. It means the structure has to serve creativity, not smother it. The Creative who finds a flexible system produces more than any forced disciplined type.

👥 Dynamics

Dynamics with other profiles

When two personalities meet, there are three possible kinds of relationship: complementarity (they complete each other), resonance (they understand each other deeply, but amplify the negative), and friction (they clash).

High Friction
Creative×Traditional

Change versus routine. You want variety; the Traditional wants predictability. Without awareness, one smothers the other.

Complementary
Creative+Intellectual

Hands-on creativity + mental creativity. Together they turn ideas into reality with depth and beauty.

Complementary
Creative+Empathetic

Creativity + connection. Both are emotional and value experiences. Together they build rich, vibrant bonds.

Resonance
Creative≈Creative

Total understanding: both get the need for novelty. Risk: they amplify the instability and neither one finishes anything.

🎯 Recalibration

Questions to recalibrate yourself

These are questions you can ask yourself regularly to check whether you're aligned, or whether the Creative's bias is distorting your decisions.

1

"Am I chasing something new because I truly need to or because I'm running from the discomfort of staying?"

2

"If I had stuck with the last project, where would I be now?"

3

"Am I mistaking natural boredom for a sign that I need to change?"

4

"What would happen if I held on for 3 more months before giving up?"

Your next step

Identify one project you abandoned before reaping the result, not because it was wrong, but out of boredom. Ask yourself honestly: "If I had stuck with it, would it have worked?" If the answer is yes, consider picking it back up. Not with the same energy you had at the start, but with the mature energy of someone who knows that staying is just as creative as starting.

This is your profile. It's not a sentence.

Being a Creative isn't instability, it's vitality. The world needs people who create, connect, and bring color. Your journey isn't about becoming predictable. It's about using your creative energy with awareness and learning that staying can be just as beautiful as starting.
